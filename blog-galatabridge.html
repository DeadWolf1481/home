Galata Bridge: The Heart of Istanbul

📅 30 April 2026

Stretching across the Golden Horn, Galata Bridge is one of Istanbul's most beloved landmarks — a place where fishermen line the railings, ferries pass below, and the city reveals itself in all directions.

History of Galata Bridge

The current Galata Bridge was built in 1994, but bridges have linked Eminönü and Karaköy here since the Byzantine era. The Ottoman-era predecessor was a famous gathering place for vendors and fortune tellers.

Fishing on the Bridge

At any hour, dozens of anglers line the railings with long rods dangling into the Golden Horn below. Watching them is one of Istanbul's great free pleasures.

The Lower Level

The bridge's lower deck is lined with restaurants and cafés. The setting — beneath the bridge with Bosphorus and minarets framing the view — is spectacular at sunset.

Getting There
