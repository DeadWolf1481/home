Sultanahmet: Istanbul's Historic Heart

📅 April 10, 2026⏱ 5 min read

If Istanbul has a historical and spiritual centre, it is Sultanahmet. This compact neighbourhood on the historic peninsula contains more world-class monuments per square kilometre than almost anywhere else on earth.

The Blue Mosque

The Sultan Ahmed Mosque — known internationally as the Blue Mosque for the İznik tiles that line its interior — was completed in 1616 and remains an active place of worship. Its six minarets caused controversy at the time of construction (only Mecca's mosque had six) and still define the Istanbul skyline today. The interior is a stunning exercise in Ottoman craftsmanship, with 20,000 hand-painted tiles and a prayer hall that floods with coloured light through 260 windows.

The Hippodrome

Directly in front of the Blue Mosque lies At Meydanı, the ancient Hippodrome of Constantinople. This was the ceremonial and sporting heart of Byzantine Constantinople — a stadium that could hold 100,000 spectators. Three ancient monuments remain: the Egyptian Obelisk (erected circa 1550 BC, brought to Constantinople in 390 AD), the Serpent Column (from Delphi, dating to 479 BC), and the Column of Constantine VII. The open square makes a pleasant gathering point between sights.

The Basilica Cistern

Beneath the streets of Sultanahmet lies one of Istanbul's most atmospheric sights — the Basilica Cistern, a vast underground Byzantine reservoir built in the 6th century. Supported by 336 ancient columns rising from a shallow layer of water, it is lit with dramatic effect and contains two intriguing Medusa heads used as column bases. The cistern was recently restored and now includes a light and sound installation.

Where to Eat in Sultanahmet

Sultanahmet's tourist density means restaurants vary widely in quality. For a reliable experience, look for places serving traditional Turkish dishes slightly away from the main square — streets like Akbıyık Caddesi and Divan Yolu have a good mix. Must-tries in the area: mercimek çorbası (red lentil soup), İskender kebab, and traditional Turkish breakfast spreads.
