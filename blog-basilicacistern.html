Basilica Cistern: Istanbul's Underground Wonder

📅 30 April 2026

Beneath the streets of Sultanahmet lies one of Istanbul's most atmospheric spaces — a vast underground Byzantine reservoir supported by 336 ancient columns, lit dramatically from below, with two mysterious Medusa heads in the depths.

History of the Cistern

Built in 532 AD under Emperor Justinian I, the Basilica Cistern could hold up to 80,000 cubic metres of water. The Ottomans rediscovered it in the 16th century when locals were found fishing through holes in their floors.

The Columns

The cistern is supported by 336 marble columns in 12 rows of 28. Most are 9 metres tall and were repurposed from older Roman buildings across the empire.

The Medusa Heads

The cistern's most famous curiosities are two enormous Medusa head column bases — one upside down, one on its side. They were likely brought from an earlier Roman monument.

Visiting Today

The Basilica Cistern has been restored and includes a dramatic light installation. Book tickets online. Open daily, fully wheelchair accessible.

Getting There

The entrance is on Alemdar Caddesi in Sultanahmet. From Istanbul Airport, a transfer takes approximately 45–60 minutes.
