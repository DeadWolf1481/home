Topkapi Palace: Heart of the Ottoman Empire

📅 April 10, 2026⏱ 7 min read

For nearly 400 years, Topkapi Palace was the administrative and residential centre of one of history's greatest empires. Today, spread across a promontory overlooking the Bosphorus, it is one of Istanbul's most rewarding museums.

The Palace That Ran an Empire

Built by Mehmed II in the 1450s and expanded by successive sultans, Topkapi served as the seat of the Ottoman government and the private residence of the sultan and his court for nearly four centuries. At its height, the palace complex housed up to 4,000 people — administrators, soldiers, servants, and the inhabitants of the famous Harem. It was the nerve centre from which an empire stretching across three continents was governed.

The Four Courtyards

Topkapi is organised around four successive courtyards, each more exclusive than the last in the palace's working days. The First Courtyard (also called the Court of the Janissaries) was open to the public. The Second housed the main administrative offices. The Third — the most intimate — contained the Sultan's private apartments, the Treasury, and the sacred relics of the Prophet Muhammad. The Fourth Courtyard offered terraced gardens and pavilions with extraordinary views.

The Treasury and Sacred Relics

The Treasury section draws large crowds, and with good reason. Among its highlights are the 86-carat Spoonmaker's Diamond, the Topkapi Dagger encrusted with emeralds, and an extraordinary collection of Chinese celadon porcelain. The Pavilion of the Sacred Relics houses items of profound importance to the Islamic world, including the mantle and sword of the Prophet Muhammad.

The Harem

The Harem — a word meaning 'forbidden' in Arabic — was the private residential quarters of the sultan, his mother, wives, concubines, and children. It consists of some 400 rooms and was designed to be entirely self-contained. Guided tours of the Harem require a separate ticket but are absolutely worth it for the spectacular İznik tile work and the ornate decoration of the Imperial Hall.

Planning Your Visit

Topkapi Palace is one of Istanbul's busiest attractions. Book tickets online in advance to avoid queuing, and allocate at least half a day — most visitors spend 3–4 hours. The palace is closed on Tuesdays. From Istanbul Airport, a private transfer to Sultanahmet takes approximately 45–60 minutes with Airports Transfer Turkey, arriving refreshed and ready to explore.
