Blue Mosque: Istanbul's Most Iconic Landmark

📅 30 April 2026

The Sultan Ahmed Mosque — known worldwide as the Blue Mosque — is one of the most recognisable buildings in the world. Its six minarets and cascading domes define the Istanbul skyline, while its interior of 20,000 hand-painted İznik tiles gives it the name that has endured for four centuries.

History of the Blue Mosque

Built between 1609 and 1616 under Sultan Ahmed I, the mosque was designed by architect Sedefkâr Mehmed Ağa. The sultan's decision to commission six minarets caused controversy — at the time, only the mosque in Mecca had six.

The İznik Tiles

The interior's blue hue comes from over 20,000 hand-painted İznik tiles. The tiles feature intricate patterns of tulips, carnations, lilies, and geometric motifs in shades of cobalt blue, turquoise, green, and white.

Visiting the Blue Mosque

The Blue Mosque remains an active place of worship. Entry is free but restricted during prayer times. Modest dress is required — shoulders and knees must be covered, and shoes must be removed.
